## Slide-by-slide brief

**Slide 1 — Cover (T01_Cover)**
Headline: "What does working with Envesty actually cost?"
Subhead: "Here's every number. Before you ask."
Tone: Confident, plainspoken. No stock people imagery — use typography on a clean background.
This is a direct counter-positioning play. The visual should feel different from the typical promo post.

**Slide 2 — BigStat (T02_BigStat)**
Stat: "₹0"
Subhead: "What we charge for the first call. You should know if we're the right fit before you pay anything."
Purpose: Immediate scroll-stopper and trust signal. Counterintuitive opening for a pricing post.

**Slide 3 — Comparison (T03_Comparison): The industry pattern vs. our approach**
Left: Low headline price, upsell calls after signing, final bill 3–5×. No competitor named — this is a behavioural description, not an attack.
Right: Free for a limited period; when we charge, every line item visible before signing.
Guardrail check: Does NOT name RegisterKaro or any competitor. Counter-positions by being transparent, not by shaming.

**Slide 4 — Comparison (T03_Comparison): Single-vendor route vs. Envesty bundle**
Left: 4 vendors, 4 contracts, 4 blame-layers when timelines slip.
Right: One team, one timeline, one accountable point of contact.
This is Envesty's core differentiator slide — earn the positioning by showing the mechanism, not just claiming it.

**Slide 5 — Quote (T04_Quote)**
Sushant's voice on why we publish numbers. Honest stage framing ("since Feb 2025").
Attribution must use real name + honest tenure.

**Slide 6 — CTA (T05_CTA)**
"Book a 20-min slot." Direct CTA from the brand kit CTA library.
Body: "We'll walk your specific case and quote a real number. No follow-up upsell calls."
The last line does extra work — it directly addresses the fear the target audience carries from previous experiences.
