# Slot 04 — GSTIN Reveal (Education Reel)
**Cycle:** 2026-06_cycle-02
**Pillar:** Education
**Format:** Reel — two-person skit + diagram cutaway
**Target duration:** ~35 seconds
**Language:** English (with Hindi subtitles recommended)

---

## Full script

**[Scene 1 — Hook, 0–5s]**
Person A: "Tell me your GSTIN."
Person B: *(reads out)* "27AABCE1234F1Z5."
Person A: "Okay — Maharashtra. Primary registration, not a branch."
Person B: "Wait — how do you know that from just a number?!"

---

**[Scene 2 — Reveal, 5–20s]**
*(Cut to clean graphic: 15 characters highlighted segment by segment)*

VO / Person A:
"Your GSTIN is 15 characters. The first two are your state code — 27 is Maharashtra, 09 is Uttar Pradesh, 07 is Delhi. Characters 3 to 12 are your PAN exactly as it's registered. Character 13 tells you if this is your primary registration in that state — that's a 1 — or a second location, which would be a 2. The last two are check digits."

*(Graphic shows each segment lighting up as described)*

---

**[Scene 3 — Why it matters, 20–30s]**
*(Back to talking head, direct to camera)*

"Every bank, vendor, and GST officer reading your GSTIN already knows your state, your entity, and whether this is a head office or a branch. Before they ask you anything. You should know what you're handing out."

---

**[Scene 4 — CTA, 30–35s]**
"Comment your state code below — I'll tell you which state it maps to. And if you're about to register for GST for the first time, reply — I'll walk you through what actually happens on the portal."

---

## Production notes

- **No stock people.** Person A and Person B should be real — ideally Sushant + a colleague or fellow founder.
- **Graphic style:** Use brand colours (#0c0e12 background, #3d7bff accent). The 15-character string should animate segment by segment. Simple, not flashy.
- **Caption on-screen:** Add Hindi subtitles for the full script — increases reach significantly (Reels 1 and 5 in the dataset both used Hindi captions to extend across language audiences).
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical.

---

## Reel analysis pattern applied
Direct adaptation of the curiosity-gap magic-reveal format from @ca.kunalkumar's PAN card reel (1.15M views, 21.9K shares).
That reel's mechanic: Person A appears to magically know private information → reveals the structural logic → audience immediately tests on their own PAN/GSTIN → comment trigger.
GSTIN is a direct parallel to the PAN structure reveal — same "hidden meaning in an official number" frame, adapted for Envesty's compliance and GST audience.
The comment-bait CTA ("comment your state code") mirrors the highest-engagement closing mechanic in the dataset.
