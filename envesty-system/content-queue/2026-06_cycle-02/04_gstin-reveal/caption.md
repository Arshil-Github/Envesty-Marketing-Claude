Your GSTIN isn't random.

Every one of the 15 characters is a code:

→ Characters 1–2: State code (09 = UP, 27 = Maharashtra, 07 = Delhi)
→ Characters 3–12: Your PAN, exactly as registered
→ Character 13: Registration type (1 = primary, 2 = second location in same state, and so on)
→ Characters 14–15: Check digits assigned by the system

Every bank, vendor, and GST officer reading your GSTIN already knows your state, your PAN, and whether this is a head office or a branch. Before they say a word.
