5 legal agreements every Indian founder must have — and most skip all of them.

The one that causes the most damage: the Co-Founder Agreement.

Your standard MOA won't cover what happens when a partner stops contributing but keeps 50% equity. You need:
→ Reverse vesting (what happens to their shares if they leave early)
→ Buy-back price formula (so there's no fight about valuation later)
→ Dispute resolution mechanism (so one disagreement doesn't deadlock the whole company)

The other four:
→ IP Assignment Agreement (domain + brand + code owned by the company, day 1)
→ Privacy Policy (DPDP Act 2023 makes this a legal requirement for Indian businesses collecting user data)
→ Terms of Service (defines what you're liable for — critical if you serve international clients)
→ Employment / Vendor NDA (work product belongs to the company when people leave)

None of these need to be expensive. They need to exist.
