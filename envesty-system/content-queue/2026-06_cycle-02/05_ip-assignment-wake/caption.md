A founder I know had a serious acquisition offer fall apart in due diligence.

The reason: their developer — who'd left 6 months earlier — still technically owned the codebase. No IP assignment agreement.

This is more common than people realise.

When you hire someone — especially a freelancer or vendor — the work they create is legally theirs by default unless there's a written agreement that says otherwise. A WhatsApp conversation doesn't transfer IP. Neither does a basic offer letter.

An IP assignment agreement fixes this. It says: everything you build for this company, while working for this company, belongs to the company.

The three clauses you need in every employment and vendor agreement:
1. Work-for-hire (all output belongs to the company)
2. Confidentiality (they can't share what they built or learned)
3. IP non-compete (the specific assets they created aren't theirs to reuse)

You don't need an expensive lawyer to add these. You need someone to make sure they're there.
