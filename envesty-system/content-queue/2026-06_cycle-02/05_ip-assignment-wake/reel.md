# Slot 05 — IP Assignment Wake-Up Call (Education Reel)
**Cycle:** 2026-06_cycle-02
**Pillar:** Education
**Format:** Reel — talking head, fear + authority, founder direct-to-camera
**Target duration:** ~60 seconds
**Language:** English (Hindi subtitles recommended)

---

## Full script

**[Scene 1 — Hook, 0–8s]**
"A founder I spoke to recently had a serious acquisition offer — call it a life-changing number. It fell apart in due diligence. The reason? The developer who built their core product — who'd left the company six months earlier — still technically owned the codebase. There was no IP assignment agreement."

---

**[Scene 2 — Explain the mechanism, 8–25s]**
"Here's how this happens. When you hire a developer — especially a freelancer or vendor — the work they create is theirs by default unless there's a written agreement that says otherwise. Most founders use a simple offer letter or a WhatsApp conversation. Neither of those transfers IP."

"An IP assignment agreement is a single document that says: everything you build for this company, while engaged with this company, belongs to the company. It takes 15 minutes to draft. It takes months to fix when it's missing."

*(Optional text overlay: 'WhatsApp conversation ≠ IP transfer')*

---

**[Scene 3 — Broaden it, 25–45s]**
"This isn't just about developers. It's your brand designer. Your co-founder who registered the domain in their personal name. Any vendor who created something for you. Any of them can walk out the door holding something that belongs to your business — unless the agreement is in writing and specific."

"The three clauses that protect you: work-for-hire, confidentiality, and non-compete on the IP they created. You don't need a ₹5 lakh lawyer to draft these. You need someone to make sure they exist."

*(Optional text overlays: 'Work-for-hire / Confidentiality / IP non-compete' as each is named)*

---

**[Scene 4 — CTA, 45–60s]**
"If you've hired anyone — developer, designer, CA, vendor — and you don't have a signed IP assignment in place, reply to this and I'll tell you exactly what to ask for. At Envesty, we include this as a standard part of every company setup we run. It should be default for every founder."

## Production notes

- **Presenter:** Sushant Choudhury, direct-to-camera. Real founder, real name — brand kit requires this.
- **No stock people.** The scenario ("a founder I spoke to") is deliberately anonymised — this is NOT a fabricated testimonial. It describes a scenario type, not a named client. Do not add a name or company to this story.
- **Tone:** Serious and empathetic, not alarmist. The founder in the story is a victim of a gap, not of negligence.
- **Graphics:** Minimal. The story carries the weight. Use text overlays only for the 3 clauses (Scene 3) to give the audience something to screenshot.
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical.

## Reel analysis pattern applied
Adapts the fear + authority format from @casarthakahuja's long-form legal reel (541K views, 41K shares):
open with a visceral "imagine if" scenario → explain the structural mechanic that causes it → broaden to the audience's situation → close with a personal, low-friction CTA.
