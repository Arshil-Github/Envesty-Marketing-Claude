What RoC-Kanpur actually checks before approving your company name.

Most founders don't know step 3 exists.

Here's the full checklist the officer runs — not what the MCA portal implies:

1. Is the name unique on the MCA company name database?
2. Does it conflict with a live trademark on IP India?
3. Does the MOA object clause describe a restricted or licensed activity? (banking, insurance, NBFC, pharma — each has a separate approval process)
4. Does the name use restricted words — India, National, Government, Bank, Exchange — without regulatory permission?
5. Is the name too generic to be distinctive? (Tech Solutions Private Limited gets flagged)

The portal accepts the form after step 1. The officer checks steps 2–5 manually.

That's where most resubmissions come from.
