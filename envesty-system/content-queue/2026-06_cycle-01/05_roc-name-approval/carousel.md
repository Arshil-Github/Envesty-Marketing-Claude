## Slide 3 — Comparison
**Headline:** Names that get rejected vs. names that sail through

| Left: Rejected — common reasons | Right: Approved — what works |
|---|---|
| Too generic: standalone India, Digital, Tech, Solutions · Sounds like a govt body or bank · Too similar to an existing MCA-registered name · Conflicts with a live IP India trademark | Coined or distinctive word + descriptor · Passes MCA name search · Passes IP India trademark check · MOA object clause doesn't conflict with restricted activities |

---

## Slide 4 — Comparison
**Headline:** The step most guides skip — the MOA object clause review

| Left: What most guides say | Right: What actually happens |
|---|---|
| File SPICe+ with your name. Wait for MCA approval. Done. | The RoC officer reads your MOA object clause manually. If it describes a restricted or licensed activity (banking, insurance, NBFC, pharma), the name rejects — even if the name itself is unique and spelt correctly. |
